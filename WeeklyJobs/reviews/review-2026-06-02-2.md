## Notes & flags

- **#1 (Bankrate) — hybrid-centric wording.** The role is genuinely "open to remote," which keeps it
  inside Rule 4 (region *preference* is allowed). But "hybrid being central to our NY / Charlotte
  offices" signals the team leans in-office. Confirm a fully-remote candidate is actually accepted
  before posting; if the listing later turns out to require office presence, flip to REJECT.
- **#3 (Ladders) — recruiter repost, undisclosed employer.** Reads as an aggregator/recruiter
  listing ("for our client"), and LinkedIn gated the full detail. The base role is remote, so it is
  **not** a Rule 4 reject — but the missing employer name + repost quality is why it's REVIEW under
  Rule 5. **Action item:** identify the real hiring company and confirm legitimacy before posting.
- **#4 (MO) — region & citizenship restrictions are allowed.** Fully remote within a 22-state list,
  US-citizenship required for the VA contract. Under Rule 4 these are region restrictions on an
  otherwise fully-remote role → PASS. Note the **2026-06-15 close date** — post promptly or skip.
- **#5 (McGraw Hill) — content/instructional design is in-niche.** "Academic Designer" is an
  instructional/curriculum-design role; per the updated Rule 6, content & instructional design
  counts as accepted design work (alongside graphic, UI/UX, branding). PASS — remote and pay were
  both fine. *(This row was initially mis-rejected as off-niche; the niche list has since been
  widened — see the [ReviewerAgent](../ReviewerAgent.md) Rule 6 update.)*
- **`workType` tag is not a verdict.** All six extracted as `REMOTE`; #1 needed the JD text read in
  full to confirm remote is genuinely offered (vs the hybrid-centric framing).
- **No in-batch duplicates.** Six distinct source URLs → six distinct listings.
